Contracting parties: 5 total: Denmark (Faroe Islands & Greenland), EU, Iceland, Norway, Russian Federation.

Area: Atlantic and Arctic Oceans.     

Date entered into force: 1982

FAO association: No.

FAO statistical area: 27.

Objectives: “…to recommend measures to maintain the rational exploitation of fish stocks in the Atlantic and Arctic Oceans.”

Primary species: Blue whiting (Micromesistius poutassou), deep-sea species, Haddock (Melanogrammus aeglefinus), Herring (Clupea harengus), Mackerel (Scomber scombrus), Redfish (Sebastes mentella).

 

The x-axes on the figures below represent either current biomass (B2007) or current spawning stock biomass (SSB2008 or SSB2006) over either biomass at maximum sustainable yield (Bmsy), spawning stock biomass at maximum sustainable yield (SSBmsy), or spawning stock biomass at “critical level” (SSBlim), or lowest observed SSB in the time-series (SSBloss). The y-axes represent current fishing mortality (F2008, F2007 or F2006) over either fishing mortality that produces maximum sustainable yield (Fmsy), or the fishing mortality limit or target (Flim or Ftarget, respectively). Shown below, are stocks: a) Blue Whiting; b) Mackerel; c) Redfish; d) Herring, Norwegian-spawning; e) Herring, Norway-Sweden stock; f) Haddock. For data sources, see Cullis-Suzuki and Pauly (2009).
